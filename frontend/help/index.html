Getting Started

How to Upload Your First Video

3 min read Beginner

Welcome to Basketball Analysis! This guide will walk you through uploading your first video for analysis.

Step 1: Record Your Shot

  • Position camera at side angle, about 10-15 feet away
  • Ensure entire body is visible from head to feet
  • Record in good lighting conditions
  • Keep camera steady (use tripod if available)

Step 2: Upload Video

  1. Log into your Basketball Analysis account
  2. Click "Upload New Video" on your dashboard
  3. Select your video file (MP4, MOV, or AVI format)
  4. Wait for upload to complete

Step 3: Start Analysis

Once uploaded, analysis will start automatically. You'll receive an email when results are ready!

Video Recording Tips

Perfect Camera Positioning

4 min read Beginner

Proper camera positioning is crucial for accurate analysis. Follow these guidelines for best results.

Camera Angle

  • Side View: Position camera directly to the side of the shooter
  • Height: Camera should be at chest/shoulder height
  • Distance: Stand 10-15 feet away from the shooter
  • Stability: Use a tripod or stable surface

Framing Guidelines

  • Entire body visible from head to feet
  • Include space above head and below feet
  • Shooter should be centered in frame
  • Avoid moving the camera during recording

Lighting and Environment

3 min read Beginner

Good lighting and environment setup improve analysis accuracy significantly.

Lighting Requirements

  • Natural Light: Outdoor recording during daylight hours
  • Indoor Lighting: Well-lit gym with even lighting
  • Avoid: Backlighting or shadows on the shooter
  • Consistency: Even lighting across the shooting motion

Background Considerations

  • Clear, uncluttered background
  • Contrasting colors to distinguish body outline
  • Avoid busy patterns or moving objects
  • Solid colored wall or court background works best

Reading Feedback Reports

4 min read Beginner

Understand how to interpret and act on the feedback provided in your analysis reports.

Feedback Categories

  • Form: Body positioning and alignment
  • Timing: Coordination and rhythm
  • Balance: Stability throughout the shot
  • Consistency: Repeatability of motion

Severity Levels

Minor: Small adjustments recommended
Moderate: Noticeable impact on accuracy
Major: Significant form correction needed

Using Recommendations

Each feedback point includes specific drills and exercises to address the identified area. Practice these consistently for best results.
